# SECTION 1: NARRATIVE ARCHITECTURE

## 1. Standout Amenity Narratives

**The Still Water** — Private Villa Pool
Each villa's pool appears at the edge of the living pavilion like a held breath — a mirror of sky, stone, and silence belonging entirely to one family. Temperature-controlled to body temperature at dusk; spectrum calibrated from amber at golden hour to deep cobalt by midnight. The Still Water becomes the villa's most intimate room — one without walls.
*Brand inspiration: Aman (pool as spiritual threshold, not recreational facility).*

**The Restoration Pavilion** — Private Spa
A free-standing structure within each villa's private garden, modelled on Japanese onsen rooms and Ottoman hammam traditions. Heated basalt floor, copper soaking tub, Portuguese slate steam room, resident therapist who learns each owner's body over years. No booking system. No menu. Only your therapist, your time, and silence so complete you hear your own heartbeat.
*Brand inspiration: Four Seasons (personalized ritual) + Aman (spa as sanctuary).*

**The Cellar Room** — Wine & Spirits Lounge
Below grade, temperature-held at 14°C, lit by a single pendant in aged brass. A retained Master of Wine curates a private lock-box of 600 bottles per family with single-vineyard bottling rights. Seven steps of honed Nero Marquina marble. By the time you reach the bottom, the city has entirely ceased to exist.
*Brand inspiration: St. Regis (champagne ritual, aristocratic theatre) + Bvlgari (Italian connoisseurship).*

**The Approach** — Arrival Motor Court
A 90-metre private driveway lined with mature rain trees opening onto a hand-laid granite forecourt. Completely private to each villa — no shared entry, no intercom visible from the street. The gate is already open. The forecourt stone already warmed. One staff member — never more — with a chilled towel and your name.
*Brand inspiration: Aman (arrival road as decompression ritual) + Binghatti (bold architectural forecourt).*

**The Green Circuit** — Landscaped Garden Walk
A 600-metre path through the estate's botanical reserve — a living collection of heritage species, medicinal plantings, and seasonal flowering specimens. Not a jogging track. At three points along the circuit, a stone bench appears beneath a canopy — no signage, no lighting column, just shade and birdsong cultivated by a resident ornithologist.
*Brand inspiration: Aman (landscape as sanctuary) + Sobha Realty (Never Compromise horticultural specification).*

**The Screening Room** — Private Cinema
Forty seats, a 6-metre curved screen, 7.1.4 Dolby Atmos, hand-stitched Italian leather seating, and an intermission ritual: a trained attendant appears at the ninety-minute mark with a champagne trolley. Residents may commission private premieres and director Q&A events through a cultural programming partnership. Available exclusively to the twelve families.
*Brand inspiration: St. Regis (the butler's instinct for timing) + Four Seasons (warm luxury).*

**The Horizon Deck** — Rooftop Sky Terrace
Forty-two feet above garden level, a full-footprint terrace with 270-degree city canopy views. Three experiences: fire table for cold evenings, cooling mist for summer nights, a twelve-seat long table for open-sky chef-catered dinners. The structural railing is a single wire of marine-grade stainless at hip height — invisible in the view, present only as safety.
*Brand inspiration: Bvlgari (Il Cielo — sky as the luxury) + Aman (horizon as design element).*

## 4. Architect Collaboration Brief

**Material palette philosophy:** Two registers — geological (travertine, basalt, aged teak showing geological memory) and artisanal (lime plaster with evident trowel technique, oxidised bronze that will patinate, honest timber joinery). References: Aman Tokyo raw plaster walls, Amanjiwo riven stone floors, Bvlgari Il Cielo Dubai oxidised copper, Four Seasons Chiang Mai teak and limestone.

**Facade mood:** Not an elevation — a garden wall with rooms behind it. Landscape dominates from the street. Fenestration minimal on public-facing elevations, generous on garden-facing faces. No painted surfaces; each material is its own colour. No polished marble in high-traffic areas.

**Arrival sequence design:** Three acts — separation from city, orientation toward nature, deceleration. Vehicular and pedestrian routes diverge before final approach. No visible signage at boundary. One stone monolith, 600mm high, deeply incised typeface. No illuminated signage. No company logos. No awards plaques.

**Indoor-outdoor flow philosophy:** The pavilion grammar, not the room. Retractable glass walls that disappear into floor channels. Covered outdoor living rooms architecturally equivalent to interiors — same ceiling height, same material quality, same furniture specification. Climate solution, not air-conditioning solution.

**Signage and wayfinding tone:** No signage system. Wayfinding through design. Where text is necessary: incised into stone or cast in oxidised bronze plate, set flush with surface. No standing signage. No illuminated directories. No arrows. Typography: single serif typeface, small capitals, sizes calibrated for the person standing in front of them, not passing by.

# SECTION 2: BRAND IDENTITY SYSTEM

## Recommended Project Name: Virāma

Sanskrit: विराम — a complete pause; the diacritical mark that signals cessation of sound; "this word is whole."

Of the five candidates evaluated — Virāma, Pietra (Italian: stone), Seren (Welsh: star), Valanor (coined Sanskrit-Persian), Alvar (Latin/Nordic: limestone plain) — Virāma is the only name that *performs* the brand promise within the act of hearing it. It does not describe a quality; it is the quality.

**Brand extension:** "Virāma by SSquare" — the developer becomes a provenance mark, not the headline.

## Logo Direction

**Form:** Combination mark — wordmark primary, logomark subordinate.

**Symbol concept:** The Threshold — a liminal geometric form suggesting passage without depicting a door. One visible layer (elegant geometric form) + one hidden layer (the number twelve embedded for the attentive eye).

**Construction:** Engraving-weight line quality. The fineness of a Patek Philippe dial or Assouline book spine. Very fine stroke weight. Generous open tracking on wordmark.

**Typography direction:** Classical serif — Freight Display Pro / Canela (Commercial Type) / Portrait (Commercial Type), or bespoke.

**Five prohibitions:**
1. No architectural symbol (house, arch, dome)
2. No gradient, shadow, or dimensional effect
3. No more than two visual elements
4. No stock typefaces or icon libraries
5. The logo explains nothing — it identifies

**The logo test:**
- *Silence Test:* Viewers say "quiet, permanent, private, rare" — not "modern, clean, tech"
- *Material Test:* Works blind-embossed on cotton-rag, hot-stamped on matte card, engraved on brass plate
- *20-Year Test:* Excellent in 2005 and 2045 — not of-this-moment

**Non-negotiable rule:** The logo is never the loudest thing in the room. It certifies. Once. Correct form. Sufficient space. Then silent.

# SECTION 3: CONTENT AND EXPERIENCE DESIGN

## Hero Narrative — Approved Copy (300 Words)

There is a kind of silence that has nothing to do with the absence of sound.

It is the silence of a room that was designed for you — not for a market, not for a segment, not for a buyer profile assembled from preference surveys. For you. The specific weight of you, arriving home on a Thursday evening, shedding the city at the threshold, walking into a space that remembers what you need before you do.

Virāma is twelve private villas. That is not a phase one. There is no phase two. There will never be another Virāma, because a thing that is truly complete cannot be added to.

Each villa occupies ground that was selected before a single architectural line was drawn — positioned for light, for stillness, for the particular quality of morning that belongs only to this address. The Still Water reflects your sky. The Restoration Pavilion exists because recovery is as serious a pursuit as achievement. The Long Table is for the evenings when the people who matter most to you are finally in the same room. These are not amenities. They are arguments for a different way of living.

SSquare began this project with a single constraint: no material, no finish, and no commitment made to the first buyer will be compromised for the twelfth. That constraint is the building's spine.

The twelve families who live here will know each other by name. Their children will share a world that was built to last. The team that maintains this address will stay for a decade, minimum — because a home is not a product, and its care cannot be outsourced to whoever wins the next contract.

Come home to quiet.

Not the quiet of emptiness. The quiet of everything being, finally, in its right place.

## Site Office Experience — Key Principles

**Exterior:** Pale plaster and dark basalt. One engraved stone panel at eye level: "Virāma. By SSquare." No hoarding, no flags. Mature tree row creates threshold before building is reached. Gravel forecourt — the sound of arrival is intentional.

**Arrival sequence:** 20-metre walk, path designed for two. Solid timber door opened by a person. Always. First sound: the door.

**Reception:** Single slab of dark book-matched marble (not a counter). Virāma Stone wall, wordmark backlit at 2700K. Temperature 22°C. In-slab floor heating. Everything returns warmth to the hand.

**Presentation room:** 98-inch screen behind frosted glass panel that lifts on pneumatic system — revealed, not switched on. Three lighting pre-sets: Welcome, Presentation, Reveal.

**Model reveal:** Separate room, never visible on arrival. Near-dark. 4m × 4m plinth at waist height. Illuminates slowly, like dawn. Consultant silent for thirty seconds after light comes up. The model is never explained before it is experienced.

**House Scent (proprietary, commissioned):**
- Top: Crushed grey stone, petrichor, cypress
- Heart: Dry sandalwood, hinoki cedar, old parchment
- Base: Black vetiver, amber resin, cold incense ash
- Deployed via HVAC at sub-detection-threshold concentration. The visitor cannot locate it. They simply find the room calming.

**Acoustic brief:** Composed 30-minute loops. Foundation layer (near-silence room tone). Environmental layer (one bird call every 4–7 minutes, distance-processed). Musical layer in presentation room only (sparse piano and cello, below conversation threshold). Model room: complete silence. The model commands the room acoustically through its own gravity.

**Leave-behind (given in the model room, not at exit):**
Clamshell box, Dark Basalt board, Arcadian Gold foil wordmark. Contents: 48-page brand book (narrative, philosophy, materials — no pricing), floor plan folio on uncoated paper (rolled, linen ribbon), handwritten note from project director (ink pen, four sentences), glass vial of house scent sealed with wax (one word engraved: "Virāma").

**Staff brief:** Two senior consultants only, for all twelve villas. No rotation. Deep knowledge, not memorised lines. The word "affordable" is never used. The word "deal" is never used. When the visitor is silent, the consultant is silent.

# 1. 60-PAGE LUXURY LAUNCH BROCHURE BRIEF

## Format, Materials & Production Specification

**Finished Size:** 260mm x 340mm (portrait, slightly oversized A4 proportions — commands presence without being unwieldy)
**Paper — Text Pages:** Fedrigoni Arcoset Extra White 150gsm for body pages; alternating with Fedrigoni Symbol Tatami 120gsm for chapter-break tissue inserts
**Paper — Cover:** 3mm grey board, case-bound, wrapped in natural Wibalin Buckram cloth (natural linen weave) in a tone matched to Sandstone Pale (#E8DDD0)
**Binding:** Swiss binding (lay-flat; pages open fully flat to honour full-bleed landscape photography)
**Special Finishes:**
- Cover: Blind deboss of the logomark (threshold geometry) centred on front board — no ink, only impression
- Cover spine: Foil stamp in Arcadian Gold (#C9A96E) — project name only, in Freight Display, fine tracking
- Chapter openers: Localised spot UV on the chapter numeral and title
- Select photography spreads: Varnish flood on image, matte varnish on all surrounding text areas — creates tactile contrast
- Paper band/belly-wrap (optional but recommended): 120gsm translucent vellum band, printed with the tagline in a single serif line, sealed with a matte wax stamp bearing the logomark

**Overriding Design Principle — One Rule Above All:**
Every page must contain more breathing room than content. White space (in this case, Pavilion White and Sandstone Pale) is the primary design element. Copy is never set wider than 55% of any page. Images never compete with type — they are always on different planes, different pages, or separated by a tissue insert. The reader must feel, at every turn, that the book has enormous confidence — the confidence of something that does not need to explain itself.

# 2. CUSTOMER BOOKING FORMS — DIGITAL & PRINT BRIEF

A booking form for an ultra-luxury property must never feel like a form. It must feel like the beginning of a relationship. Every field label, every divider line, every button must carry the same tone as the brochure. The form is not administrative — it is the first act of belonging.

**Rule:** No field should feel like it is interrogating the buyer. Every instruction should be phrased as an invitation, not a requirement.

---

## DIGITAL BOOKING FORM

### UX Flow — 4-Screen Progression (Not a Single Long Scroll)

**Design Philosophy for Digital:**
The digital form uses a step-by-step approach — not because it is a checkout funnel, but because each section deserves the user's full attention. A buyer spending Rs 15 Cr should never feel like they are filling out a contact-us form. The digital experience is slow, deliberate, unhurried.

**Screen 0 — Entry Screen (Pre-Form Gate):**
Full-screen immersive landing — the hero photograph (the villa entrance at golden hour from Chapter 8 of the brochure). Centred: the Virāma wordmark in Pavilion White. Beneath it, in fine tracked serif: "Register Your Interest." A single button, minimal, no border — just the text in Arcadian Gold with a fine underline. No other page elements.

**Screen 1 — Personal Details (25% progress):**

- Date of Birth

- Email Address

**Screen 4 — Success State (100%):**
No confetti. No popup. No "Thank you, someone will be in touch!"

A full-screen transition to the near-black (Deep Basalt) background. The Virāma logomark at centre, rendered in Arcadian Gold. Below it, in Freight Display, Pavilion White, 24pt:

"Your interest has been noted."

Below that, in 14pt light weight:
"A member of the Virāma team will reach you within 24 hours. You will receive a confirmation to [email]. This is not a binding booking — simply the beginning of a conversation."

A single fine-print line at foot: RERA number and project website link.

No button to return to homepage. The screen sits. It allows the moment to be a moment.

### Trust Signals (Digital Form)
- Developer logo and RERA number visible at all times in the form footer, never scrolling out of view
- SSL indicator handled through standard HTTPS — no need to call attention to it (it's table stakes, not a trust signal for this buyer)
- Testimonial or quote: Not appropriate in this form flow — it cheapens the experience. The form itself is the trust signal.
- "12 Villas. Currently [X] expressions of interest received." — a dynamic counter, if technically feasible, displayed only on Screen 0. Scarcity without pressure.

# 3. EMAIL SIGNATURE BRIEF

## Governance Principle

Every member of the Virāma / SSquare team should have an identical signature except for the name and title fields. Signatures that differ in font, layout, or colour break the brand in every email sent. This must be enforced centrally — a single HTML file distributed and locked.

## Information Hierarchy (Top to Bottom)

**Line 1:** Full name — Freight Display or web-safe equivalent (see font stack), 15px, Virāma Stone (#2C2825)
**Line 2:** Title / Role — same typeface, 11px, 40% opacity of Virāma Stone
**Separator:** A horizontal rule, 120px wide, 0.5px, Arcadian Gold (#C9A96E) — left-aligned. Not full-width.
**Line 3:** "Virāma by SSquare" — 10px, tracking +60, Virāma Stone, small caps treatment via letter-spacing
**Line 4:** Mobile number — 10px, Virāma Stone
**Line 5:** Email address — 10px, linked, underline on hover only, Virāma Stone (not blue hyperlink default — this must be overridden in the HTML)
**Separator 2:** The same Arcadian Gold hairline rule, 120px
**Logo block:** The Virāma combination mark (wordmark + logomark) — displayed at 140px wide, linked to the project website. Retina-ready (2x image file). Dark version of the logo (Deep Basalt wordmark) on white email backgrounds.
**Line 6:** RERA compliance line — 8px, 50% opacity Virāma Stone: "RERA Reg. No. [XXXX] | [State] RERA | [Project website]"
**Line 7 (optional, only for sales team):** "Virāma — 12 Private Villas | Rs 13–20 Cr | [City]" — 8px

## Font Stack (Web-Safe Equivalent)
Freight Display is not universally available in email clients. The CSS font-family stack:

```
font-family: 'Freight Display', 'Palatino Linotype', 'Palatino', 'Book Antiqua', Georgia, serif;
```

Palatino Linotype is the closest available system serif with classical proportions. Georgia is the safe fallback. Under no circumstances should the fallback cascade to Arial, Helvetica, or any sans-serif.

## Link Styling
All hyperlinks: `text-decoration: none; color: inherit;` — links inherit the text colour and carry no underline in default state. On hover: `text-decoration: underline; color: #C9A96E;` (Arcadian Gold underline). This requires `<style>` block in the HTML — which some email clients strip. Test in Outlook, Gmail, and Apple Mail. Where style stripping occurs, the plain-text fallback remains acceptable.

## Banner / Visual Element
An optional footer banner image — 600px wide x 80px tall — displaying the project's most beautiful exterior photograph cropped to a panoramic strip, with the tagline overlaid in Pavilion White: "Come Home to Quiet." This banner:
- Is a linked image (href: project website)
- Should not appear in internal emails (IT to configure rule if possible — at minimum, train the team)
- Is appropriate in all client-facing outbound communications

## Size Constraints
- Total signature HTML file: under 30KB
- Banner image: under 100KB (use WebP with JPEG fallback)
- Logo image: SVG preferred; PNG retina fallback at 2x
- Total rendered signature width: 600px max (matches standard email width)
- Mobile: The signature must remain readable at 320px — stack to single column if needed. The logo should scale down to 100px width on mobile.

## Consistency Rules for the Team
1. Signatures are distributed as a pre-built HTML file. Nobody builds their own.
2. Only name, title, and mobile number fields are editable. Everything else is locked in the HTML.
3. No personal additions — no motivational quotes, no social media icons, no animated GIFs, no personal photographs.
4. Social media links are not present in the signature — Virāma does not have a public social presence at launch. If a project Instagram account is created post-launch, one icon (the Instagram logo, rendered in Virāma Stone, 16px) may be added as a single small icon at the end of the RERA line.

# 4. TEASER HOARDING CAMPAIGN — 3-PART SERIES

## Campaign Architecture

The three hoardings function as a narrative in three acts. They are not simultaneous — they replace each other in sequence. A buyer who encounters all three has experienced a complete story. A buyer who encounters only one has still encountered something memorable.

**Core Constraint:** No product photography in any of the three teasers. No villa image. No floor plan. No price. The image never explains — it evokes.

---

## HOARDING 01 — "THE TEASE"
**Duration in Market:** Weeks 1–4 (4 weeks alone before Hoarding 02 appears)

### Headline Copy
Set on the board, typeset — not as part of the image:

> **"Soon, twelve families will live in complete quiet."**

Set in Freight Display (or equivalent large-format output equivalent), white on near-black. Below it, in the finest possible tracking, at 30% the size of the headline:

> "Virāma. By SSquare."

Nothing else.

### Visual Concept
The entire board background is a single, extreme-close-up photograph of a still water surface — the colour of early morning, before the sun has fully risen. The water occupies the full bleed. The headline is set in white, centred vertically, two lines, the larger line (the headline copy) at approximately one-sixth of the board height. Abundant negative space above and below the type.

### Design Treatment
Background: The water photograph is printed dark — pre-dawn blue-grey, almost monochrome. It reads from a distance as near-black. The headline in white is the only communication element. No logo other than the fine-print attribution at the foot. No RERA (not required at teaser stage — confirm with legal). No contact number.

Typographic hierarchy: Headline at display size. Attribution line at 15–20% of headline size. The disproportion is intentional — the headline is the only thing you can read while driving past at 60 km/h, and that is the only thing that needs to be read.

### Placement Logic
**Format:** Large-format roadside hoarding, 40ft x 20ft minimum. Placed on arterial roads with high UHNI traffic density — the route from the international airport to the business district, the approach road to premium residential neighbourhoods, the road leading past premium golf clubs and five-star hotels.

**Buyer Journey Moment:** This person has not yet considered Virāma. They may know the developer name or they may not. The communication asks for nothing except that they register the name and feel something. The word "twelve" does the heavy lifting — exclusivity communicated by number, not by adjective.

### Production Specification
- Substrate: Flex banner, 440gsm, UV-resistant ink
- Illumination: Backlit or front-lit depending on site — night visibility is non-negotiable given the UHNI audience travels to and from airports at night
- Printing: Solvent or eco-solvent wide-format, minimum 720dpi at viewing distance
- Safety: All structural specs to comply with local municipal hoarding regulations
- Mounting: On primary steel structure or building facade as per site permit

---

## HOARDING 02 — "THE STIR"
**Duration in Market:** Weeks 5–10 (replaces Hoarding 01, 6 weeks)

### Headline Copy
> **"Not a development."**
> **"Not a phase."**
> **"Twelve villas."**
> **"Final."**

Four lines. Each line on its own typographic row, with increasing weight of certainty. The last word — "Final." — sits alone, in the largest point size of the four, followed by a full stop that is the visual equivalent of the virāma mark itself.

Below all four lines, in fine tracking:
> "Virāma. Now receiving expressions of interest."

### Visual Concept
Full-bleed image: An architectural abstraction — the corner of a stone wall meeting a concrete ceiling, shot in dramatic side light so that the shadow is as interesting as the object. The photograph is monochromatic. There are no people, no context indicators, no furnishings. It is purely about material and light.

Alternatively (if architectural photography is not yet available): A macro photograph of the Virāma logo letterpress-printed into paper — the impression in the surface, photographed raking light to make the emboss visible as shadow. The brand as object.

### Design Treatment
Background: The architectural macro photograph, printed full bleed, very dark overall — at least 60% of the image reads as shadow. The four-line headline is set in Pavilion White (#F5F1EC), left-aligned, beginning at the horizontal centre of the board, with ample white space to the left — the type floats in the lighter portion of the image.

The four lines are set at slightly different sizes: each line is 10% larger than the one before it, so "Final." is noticeably the largest. This is intentional visual construction of inevitability.

### Placement Logic
**Format:** Same large-format roadside hoardings as Hoarding 01 — same sites, same boards. Replace the artwork in place. A driver who saw "twelve families will live in complete quiet" now sees "not a development. Not a phase." The sequence rewards the repeat encounter.

**Additional placement for Hoarding 02:** Airport advertising (premium format — departure lounge digital or static, business class check-in area). By week 5, the name has been introduced. The airport placement targets NRI buyers and frequent international travellers — the exact demographic for whom "twelve villas, final" resonates most acutely.

**Buyer Journey Moment:** This person may have seen Hoarding 01. They are now slightly aware. Hoarding 02 confirms what they sensed — this is rare — and adds the actionable information: expressions of interest are now open.

### Production Specification
Same substrate and print spec as Hoarding 01. For airport placements:
- Format: Tension fabric lightbox, 3m x 2m, backlit LED
- Graphic: Same design adapted to 3:2 format — the four-line headline re-composed for the smaller, more intimate airport viewing distance
- Regulation: Airport advertising placement through relevant concessionaire (DIAL for Delhi, MIAL for Mumbai, BIAL for Bangalore, etc.)

---

## HOARDING 03 — "THE INVITATION"
**Duration in Market:** Weeks 11–18 (launch period; 8 weeks)

### Headline Copy
Full-bleed, large, the entire board is type:

> **"Come Home to Quiet."**

Beneath, at half the size:
> "Virāma."

Beneath, at one-quarter the size:
> "12 Private Villas. Rs 13–20 Cr. Expressions of interest now closed for the waiting list."

Beneath, at the smallest size (but still legible from 20 metres):
> "Contact: [Name], [Mobile], [Email] | SSquare | RERA No. [XXXX]"

### Visual Concept
This is the only hoarding with no photograph. The entire board is typographic. Background: Deep Basalt (#1A1A18) — the darkest brand colour. All type in Pavilion White (#F5F1EC), except "Virāma." which is set in Arcadian Gold.

The tagline "Come Home to Quiet." is set at the maximum possible size — the letters should be legible from 100 metres. The typeface (Freight Display or production-equivalent serif) carries the weight. The full stop after "Quiet" is critical — it must be present and it must be sized proportionally (many large-format typesetters drop or minimise punctuation; this must not happen here).

### Design Treatment
Typography-only boards in this category are almost never done in Indian real estate — which is exactly why this works. Every competitor's board has a rendering, a price, a tagline over a hero shot. This board has words. At this scale, words feel like a statement, a claim, a manifesto. The buyer approaching this board reads it almost as a building facade — it has the weight of architecture.

The compositional rule: the tagline sits at the upper 40% of the board. "Virāma." sits centred between the tagline and the bottom 20% where the contact details live. There is air — a full 10% blank band — between the tagline and the wordmark, and between the wordmark and the contact block.

### Placement Logic
**Format:** Same arterial roadside hoardings. Additionally, for Hoarding 03:
- Premium mall exterior hoarding (Select City Walk, DLF Emporio, Palladium equivalent in project city)
- Building-wrap on a prominent commercial building near the project site — if available; this at Hoarding 03 scale is a landmark in itself
- Digital outdoor media (DOOH) in business districts — same design, 10-second static display in rotation

**Buyer Journey Moment:** This person has heard about Virāma. They know the name. Now the tagline lands with full weight because they understand what it means. The note about "waiting list" is the final urgency signal — deployed only at the launch phase, not before.

# 5. PRINT ADVERTISEMENTS — FULL PAGE & HALF PAGE

## Publication Strategy

**Target Publications:**
- **The Economic Times — Weekend Edition (Wealth & Luxury supplement):** Primary placement. The UHNI business reader, C-suite, promoter-class. Full page, front section or wealth supplement cover.
- **Vogue India / Harper's Bazaar India:** NRI buyer and aspirational UHNI — brand awareness, luxury context alignment. Full page inside front section.
- **Business Standard — Weekender:** High-net-worth professional reader. Full page.
- **Architectural Digest India:** Design-literate buyer. Full page. This placement is particularly important — the AD reader is a self-identified aesthete who will judge the brand by the quality of the advertisement itself.
- **Forbes India / Inc42 Founders Edition:** Entrepreneurial wealth. Half page — this reader skims, but remembers. Half page in this context is more efficient than full page.
- **NRI-targeted publications (Times of India international edition, Khaleej Times India supplement):** Half page — reaching the diaspora investor.

**Non-Negotiable Visual Rule for All Print Ads:**
Every print advertisement, full page or half page, must contain more white space than content. The advertisement must be immediately distinguishable from every other advertisement on the page, not through colour or complexity, but through quiet. If the surrounding ads are loud, the Virāma ad is the one that stops you because of what it is not doing.

---

## FULL PAGE ADVERTISEMENT BRIEF (220mm x 285mm or publication trim)

### Headline Options — Full Page (write 3, choose 1 per placement)

**Option A:**
> "Twelve villas.
> Twelve conversations.
> None of them about availability."

*(Registers scarcity with social confidence — speaks to the UHNI who negotiates everything and appreciates the reversal)*

**Option B:**
> "The rarest thing in a city this loud

*(Poetic register — appropriate for AD India, Vogue; appeals to the design-literate reader)*

**Option C:**
> "Not a floor.
> Not a tower.
> Twelve private villas.
> One of them is yours,
> if it is."

*(Conditional close — the "if it is" is the hook. The hesitation performs confidence. Appropriate for ET Wealth, Forbes India)*

### Visual Treatment — Full Page
The visual occupies the upper 65% of the page. It is a single, carefully chosen interior or architectural photograph — the same level of restraint as the brochure. Below the image: a white block (Pavilion White or printed white, matching the publication's paper as closely as possible — note to studio: always proof on the actual publication stock before finalising).

In the white block:
- The selected headline, set in Freight Display, at approximately 22–26pt, tracked elegantly, left-aligned, occupying the left 60% of the white block
- On the right 40% of the white block: the Virāma combination mark (wordmark + logomark) right-aligned to the trim edge
- A single hairline rule in Arcadian Gold running full width between the image and the white block — 0.25pt, printed as a special spot colour Pantone 871 C (metallic gold, closest match) or closest CMYK equivalent, accepted that CMYK gold is always a compromise

Below the wordmark, bottom-right:
- Contact: name and mobile number
- Below contact: RERA number, in 7pt
- SSquare name, 8pt

### Body Copy Approach — Full Page
Body copy is optional and should be used only in ET Wealth and Forbes India placements — publications where readers are slower, more text-engaged. When used: maximum 40 words. Single short paragraph. It completes the headline thought but does not explain it. Example, following Option C: "Virāma is twelve private villas, designed for twelve specific lives. If yours is among them, you will know. Expressions of interest: [contact]."

In Vogue India and AD India: no body copy. Headline + image + identity block only.

# 6. SINGLE-SCROLL SEO-FRIENDLY PROJECT WEBPAGE

## Technical Philosophy
The webpage is not a digital brochure. It is not a property listing. It is a brand experience that happens to contain information. Every section must first deliver an emotional register before it delivers data. The scroll must feel like walking through the property — a slow, considered approach.

- Image delivery: WebP with JPEG fallback; all images lazy-loaded below the fold
- Font loading: System font fallback rendered immediately; brand typeface loaded via `font-display: swap`

Before anyone reads a word, before they open the box, before they see the name — they must know that this is not ordinary. **Box dimensions:** 220mm (W) x 160mm (H) x 60mm (D) — portrait orientation, the proportion of a hardback book

## Quality Approval Protocol

**Approval Sequence:** Client review follows a three-pass system.
- **Pass 1 (Studio):** Render studio delivers greyscale/clay renders for composition and camera angle approval before colour and lighting pass.
- **Pass 2 (Colour + Light):** Full colour renders delivered. Internal review against palette reference card. Any material deviation is flagged with specific Pantone/hex comparison.
- **Pass 3 (Final):** Signed off by Creative Director and Developer principal. No changes after Pass 3 sign-off without a revision fee and timeline extension.

**Rejection Criteria (any one triggers rejection):**
- Material colours deviate more than ±5% from specified hex values under D65 illuminant
- Lens distortion visible (barrel or pincushion)
- People or figures appear identifiable (face, tattoo, distinctive clothing)
- Sky is overdramatic — purple, orange explosion, or HDR treatment visible
- Any furniture, vehicle, or prop carries visible branding
- Grain or compression artefacts visible at 100% zoom in print-resolution export
- Any render indistinguishable from competitor luxury project imagery in a blind test

## Music Brief

**Emotional arc:** The music must move from absence → presence → resonance → silence. It should not swell. It should not resolve in a traditional major-key climax. It should end quieter than it began.

**Instrumentation:** Solo piano (upright or grand, slightly room-recorded — not a studio dry sound), joined by solo cello. Optional: single Himalayan singing bowl struck once and allowed to decay naturally. No synthesisers. No strings section. No percussion of any kind.

**Tone:** Think: Nils Frahm's quieter works (*Says*, *All Melody*), Ólafur Arnalds (*Near Light*, *Near Light*), Johann Johannsson (*The Sun's Gone Dim*), Max Richter (*On the Nature of Daylight*). Do not use any of these tracks — use them as emotional reference only.

**Tempo:** No fixed BPM. Rubato — the musician controls the timing. The music must breathe, not pulse.

**Bespoke composition required:** This music must be original and owned by SSquare entirely. Commission a single Indian composer with classical + contemporary crossover experience.

**One prohibition:** The music must never feel like a real estate advertisement. If it could play over an ad for any other product, it is wrong.

## Planting and Landscape Integration

The signage must not stand alone. Every external signage element is integrated with planting.

- **Approach Marker plinth:** Surrounded by a 600mm radius ground cover planting bed. Low, dark-leafed ground cover (Ophiopogon planiscapus 'Nigrescens' or equivalent). No flowers. The stone emerges from darkness.
- **Facade Name Board wall:** The wall below the name board is preceded (at 2 metres from wall face) by a row of clipped hedgerow at 800mm height — Buxus or Duranta hedge in The Green Circuit palette (#3D4A3A). Creates a visual frame approaching the building.
- **Hoarding base:** A continuous 400mm deep gravel border with low grass. Not a planting bed — a clean separation between hoarding and road. Gravel in Sandstone Pale tone.
- **Entry Pylons:** Each pylon flanked by two specimen ornamental grasses (Miscanthus sinensis 'Gracillimus' or Pennisetum) — tall, feathery, moving in wind. Planted 300mm from pylon base. These grasses move. The pylons do not.

## Five Prohibitions — What This Facade Must NOT Look Like

**Prohibition 1: No renders on the exterior.**
The project does not advertise itself with images of what it promises to look like. Any competitor luxury project in India plasters its hoarding with photorealistic renders. Virāma uses language only. The absence of images communicates confidence — this project does not need to convince you with a picture of a pool.

**Prohibition 2: No pricing on any external signage.**
Rs 13–20 Crore is not a secret, but it is not a banner headline. Price appears nowhere on the exterior — not on the hoarding, not on the plinth, not on the facade board. This signals that Virāma selects its buyers; it does not attract them.

**Prohibition 3: No backlit panels, LED channel letters, or commercial-grade illuminated signs.**
LED channel letters are the visual language of retail and commercial property. Any illumination at Virāma is concealed, recessed, and warm. The building glows — it does not advertise.

**Prohibition 4: No QR codes, website URLs, or social media handles on the hoarding or external signage.**
Virāma is accessed by personal introduction, not by scanning a code from a car window. The URL and contact information exist in the press note, in the broker brief, and in targeted digital placements — not carved into the stone. This is a filter, not an oversight.

**Prohibition 5: No flags, bunting, balloons, temporary pull-up banners, or launch-period temporary signage.**
At no point during the pre-launch, launch, or post-launch period may any temporary branded display material appear on the external facade or boundary. The marketing office looks the same on launch day as it does six months later. Permanence is the message. Temporary materials contradict it at the first glance.

# SECTION 6: LAUNCH EVENT CONCEPTUALISATION

## Event Concept Name and Theme

**Event Name:** "The First Evening at Virāma"
**Theme:** This is not a launch event. This is the first gathering of a community that does not yet know it is a community. The evening is designed to feel like a private dinner at someone's extraordinary home — not a sales presentation. The presentation happens, but it happens inside an experience that the guests are already living.

The theme is arrival. The guests arrive at what Virāma feels like. The sell is experiential — not a deck, not a brochure walk-through. By the end of the evening, the guests understand Virāma not because they were told about it, but because they lived a version of it for four hours.

---

## Venue Type and Configuration

**Venue:** A heritage haveli, private estate, or architecturally significant private bungalow in the launch city (Mumbai or Delhi). Not a hotel ballroom. Not a convention centre. Not a developer's show flat.

**Preferred venue profile:** A privately-owned property of genuine architectural and historical significance. A 1930s–1970s bungalow with original stone and plaster. A haveli in Old Delhi with original courtyard. A pre-independence estate with mature garden. The venue must have the material weight that Virāma aspires to.

**Configuration:**
- **The Approach** (exterior, arrival zone): First 15 minutes, drinks, no seating.
- **The Threshold** (entrance hall or veranda): Transition zone. Virāma Directory discreetly placed.
- **The Presentation Chamber** (main room): 60 seats maximum. Seats arranged around a central long table, not in rows. The presentation is given from the table, not from a stage.
- **The Long Walk** (corridor or garden passage): Between arrival and presentation. Virāma collateral displayed gallery-style.
- **The Private Rooms** (2–3 side rooms): For post-presentation one-on-one conversations between developer and individual families.

**Capacity:** 60 guests maximum (approximately 24–30 families/couples, plus developer team, architect, and selected media — maximum 2 journalists, invited by name, embargoed).

## Invitee Profile and List Construction

**Invitee Categories:**

1. **Tier 1 — Pre-qualified UHNI families (direct):** 15–18 families. Net worth Rs 100 Cr+. Profile: family-owned business principals, senior corporate families (MD/CEO level), successful second-generation business leaders. Age 38–65. Families with existing second-home ownership in Alibaug, Goa, Lonavala — who understand the market and are ready to upgrade.

2. **Tier 2 — NRI investors (via family office or NRI desk):** 4–6 families. Based in UAE, UK, USA, Singapore. In India during launch period or invited with travel facilitation. Profile: successful NRI professional families with strong India emotional connection. Not purely investment-motivated — they want a home in India.

3. **Tier 3 — Channel partner principals (broker network):** 6–8 principals only. Not sales teams — principals of the top luxury residential brokerages. Invited as peers, not vendors. They are given no sales brief during the evening — they experience it as guests.

4. **Tier 4 — Selected media (embargoed):** 2 journalists, both personally invited by name. One from Architectural Digest India (editorial, not advertising desk). One from Mint Lounge or Condé Nast Traveller India. Their coverage is post-event, under embargo, and is editorial — not advertorial.

**List construction process:** Developer principal reviews and personally approves every name. No list purchased. No bulk invite. Each invitation is delivered by hand or by personal WhatsApp from the developer's own number, not from a marketing service.

## Guest Experience Journey: Arrival to Departure

### 6:30 PM — Arrival and The Approach

Guests are met at the gate by a member of the Virāma team — not a hired events hostess, but someone from the SSquare core team, trained and briefed. They are greeted by name. Each guest receives a small wrapped stone object (a smooth piece of Virāma Stone, polished, the size of a palm) as they enter. No words of explanation are offered for this object. It will make sense later.

### 6:45 PM — The Long Walk

Guests are informally guided (no announcement, no herding) through a passage or gallery corridor where the Virāma story unfolds in gallery-style format. No brochures. No QR codes. The following are displayed, framed museum-quality:

Guests are seated at The Long Table (or equivalent). No podium. No PowerPoint presentation on a screen. The developer stands at the end of the table and speaks.

2. **What we built.** (10 minutes — the architect speaks here. The walkthrough video plays on a single, large, beautifully framed screen — not a projector — at the far end of the room. Full audio.)

As dinner continues, the developer team circulates and individually invites families to a brief private conversation in one of the side rooms. These conversations last 10–15 minutes each. This is not a hard sell — it is a quiet, personal "what questions do you have" conversation. A physical site plan and villa unit overlay is available in these rooms for reference.
