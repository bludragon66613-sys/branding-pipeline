About SSquare
SSquare was founded by Vardaan and Hurmat Sarna with one conviction: that India's finest homes have not yet been built.

Not in the sense of materials or price — but in the sense of genuine intention. Too many luxury projects are sold on amenities and area. We set out to build something quieter and harder to replicate: homes designed around real privacy, real light, and the kind of stillness that changes how you live.

Every project under SSquare will be small by design. Fewer homes. More considered. We are not in the business of scale. We are in the business of getting it exactly right.

Virāma is the beginning of that proof.
— Vardaan & Hurmat Sarna, Founders

The Concept
There is a kind of silence that has nothing to do with the absence of sound.
It is the silence of a room that was designed for you —
not for a market. Not for a segment.
For the specific weight of you, arriving home on a Thursday evening,
shedding the city at the threshold.
Scale
Not selling units. Curating a community. Twelve villas. That is not a phase one. That is the whole project.
Service
Permanent estate team. Resident chef. Dedicated concierge per family. Master of Wine. Contractual — ten years post-possession.
Permanence
No phase two. No adjacent commercial plot. The twelve-villa structure makes expansion architecturally impossible.
Materials
No substitution after design approval. Designed to a standard — then built to it. What you see is what you receive.
Legacy
The twelve founding families become the permanent character of this address. Structured, governed, and covenanted to last.

Shared Community
Six Worlds Shared Between Twelve
01
The Wellspring
Wellness Pavilion
A source, not a facility. The place recovery begins. You cross a floor-level water rill of raw travertine to enter — a deliberate threshold between the estate and stillness.
02
The Long Table
Social Dining Terrace
Civilisation's oldest social technology. One handcarved teak table, seats all twelve families. Communal dinners twice yearly — winter solstice and midsummer. Menu unpublished in advance.
03
The Archive
Curated Library
A collection with intention. Architecture, Indian history, world literature, rare first editions. Each family contributes one book on possession day with a handwritten note. The registry stays open.
04
The Threshold
Arrival Plaza
Not an entrance — a moment of transition. Centrepiece: a site-specific sculpture by an Indian artist of international standing. No plaque. No explanation. The work stands on its own.
05
The Gallery Walk
Garden Art Path
600 metres where landscape and curated art dissolve into one another. Annual sculpture rotation. After 9pm, oxidised copper luminaires illuminate from below — the garden becomes a private museum after hours.
06
The Bureau
Concierge Lounge
Where things of consequence are handled by people who know how. One dedicated contact per family. A private brief on preferences, travel, occasions. Anticipation as the highest form of hospitality.
Five Brand Claims
What Virāma Does Not Share With Anyone Else
01
Scale
"We are not in the business of selling units. We are in the business of curating a community."
Twelve villas. Not a phase one. The whole project. Where competitors count towers and floors, Virāma counts families.
02
Service
"We have retained an estate management team, contractually, for ten years post-possession. The service is the product."
Not outsourced to whoever wins the next facilities contract. Permanent. Named. Accountable to the twelve families, no one else.
03
Permanence
"There is no phase two. No adjacent plot. The twelve-villa structure makes expansion architecturally impossible."
Your investment cannot be diluted by a second phase. Your address cannot be changed by what gets built next door. It is structurally protected.
04
Materials
"No material substitution is permitted after design approval. We designed to a standard and then built to it."
What you see in the show apartment is what you receive on possession. Zero substitution. Contractual. Unconditional.

Presentation Suite
The First Room of the House
Visitor Journey
Step 01
The Exterior
— 60 seconds —
Pale plaster and dark basalt. One engraved stone panel. No hoarding. Gravel forecourt. The sound of arrival is intentional.
Step 02
The Arrival
— 3–5 minutes —
A solid timber door opened by a person. Always. The first sound: the door. Temperature 22°C. In-slab floor heating. Everything returns warmth to the hand.
Step 03
The Presentation
— 25–35 minutes —
98" screen behind frosted glass — revealed on a pneumatic system, not switched on. Three lighting pre-sets. Screen shows only Virāma content.
Step 04
The Model Reveal
— 8–10 minutes —
Separate room. Near-dark. The model illuminates slowly — like dawn. Consultant silent for 30 seconds after light comes up.
House Scent
Commissioned from specialist Indian fragrance house. Deployed via HVAC — sub-detection threshold. Top: crushed grey stone, petrichor, cypress. Heart: dry sandalwood, hinoki cedar, old parchment. Base: black vetiver, amber resin, cold incense ash.
Acoustic Brief
Composed 30-minute soundscape. One bird call every 4–7 minutes, 80m distance-processed. Sparse piano and cello below conversation threshold in presentation room. Model room: complete silence only.
Hospitality
Single-origin tea or espresso, prepared behind a discreet panel. Two confections from a specific local patissier. A pre-arrival preference call collects all dietary requirements. Alcohol available on request — never offered first.
The Leave-Behind
Clamshell box, Dark Basalt board, Arcadian Gold foil wordmark. Contents: 48-page brand book (no pricing), floor plan on uncoated paper, handwritten note from project director (ink pen, four sentences), glass vial of house scent sealed with wax.
Staff Brief
Two senior consultants for all twelve villas. No rotation. No script. Deep knowledge. "Affordable," "deal," and "as per your budget" are banned. When the visitor is silent, the consultant is silent.
