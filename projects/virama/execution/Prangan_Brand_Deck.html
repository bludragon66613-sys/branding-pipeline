The Architecture
PRANGAN begins with a courtyard, because certain forms still know more about living in India than imported typologies do. Shade, breeze, stone, earth, and filtered rain shape a daily rhythm that feels familiar without feeling backward.

The architecture is contemporary, but the intelligence is inherited. Here, domestic life expands naturally toward the court, the verandah, the threshold, and back again.
Primary: Cormorant Garamond / Secondary: Inter / Accent: DM Mono
Photography Rule: No faces. Hands, thresholds, surfaces, shadows only.
The Rain Court
A central void designed to catch the monsoon. Stone underfoot, filtered light, and a dedicated space where the environment dictates the internal rhythm of the house.
The Jali Walk
A transitional corridor defined by intricate ochre jali screens. It regulates breeze and casts shifting, geometric shadows across the earth plaster throughout the day.
The Neem Commons
A shared estate gathering space shaded by mature canopy. Designed for the multigenerational household, offering a rooted, communal scale of luxury.
