# SSquare Unveils Virāma: Twelve Private Villas Conceived as an Act of Permanence

**A Rs 13–20 Crore ultra-luxury residential community for twelve founding families — where the waiting list ends when the twelfth villa is chosen.**

---

> "For thirty-five years, we have built for institutions, for commerce, for the city. Virāma is the first time we have built for a family. Twelve of them, precisely. That number was not a constraint — it was the design."
>
> — SSquare

---

**HYDERABAD, [Launch Date]** — SSquare, one of India's most enduring commercial real estate developers, has announced the launch of Virāma — a collection of twelve private villas situated in Prashashan Nagar, Jubilee Hills, Hyderabad. Priced between Rs 13 crore and Rs 20 crore, Virāma represents SSquare's first residential undertaking in a thirty-five-year career defined by the construction of over ten million square feet of commercial space. The project is not positioned within the Indian residential luxury market. It is positioned against a different standard entirely: Aman, Bvlgari Residences, Four Seasons Private Residences — properties where the measure of quality is not price per square foot, but the irreversibility of every decision made on behalf of the resident.

Virāma takes its name from the Sanskrit विराम — the diacritical mark that signals a word is complete, that no further character is required. The name is not incidental. There will be no second phase. No expansion. No additional towers conceived after launch to absorb demand. When the twelfth villa is sold, Virāma closes permanently. This is not a scarcity mechanism deployed for marketing purposes. It is a structural commitment: the entire estate — its staffing model, its maintenance protocols, its material guarantees — has been calibrated for exactly twelve households, and it cannot be diluted without being destroyed. The twelve families who choose Virāma will be its founding community and its final one.

The estate is designed to be inhabited quietly. Each villa is served by a 10-year retained estate management team — contractual and in-house, not outsourced to a third-party facilities provider — whose continuity is guaranteed beyond the handover date. Materials and finishes are locked at design approval; SSquare has committed, by contract, to zero substitution after that point. The shared amenities of Virāma have been given names rather than category labels: The Still Water (private pool), The Restoration Pavilion (spa), The Cellar Room (wine lounge), The Approach (arrival garden), The Green Circuit (garden trail), The Screening Room, and The Horizon Deck. Community spaces — exclusive to the twelve families — include The Wellspring, The Long Table, The Archive, The Threshold, The Gallery Walk, and The Bureau. These are not amenity checklists. They are rooms in an estate that twelve families will share, at a scale where privacy and community are not in tension.

## Five Key Highlights

- **Absolute limitation by design.** Virāma comprises exactly twelve villas. There is no phase two, no waitlist carried forward, no mechanism for expansion. The community closes when the twelfth villa is chosen.

- **Contractual material integrity.** Zero material substitution is permitted after design approval. This is not a brand promise — it is a legal obligation that travels with the sale deed.

- **A decade of retained estate management.** A dedicated, in-house estate team is contractually committed to Virāma for ten years post-handover. The team is not reassigned between projects. They stay.

- **SSquare's residential debut after 35 years.** A developer with ten million square feet of commercial delivery and no prior residential project. The standards that governed their institutional work — precision, durability, accountability — govern Virāma.

- **Benchmarked against global private residence standards.** Virāma's reference points are Aman, Bvlgari Residences, and Four Seasons Private Residences. The comparison is not aspirational. It is operational.

## About the Architecture and Design Direction

Virāma's architectural language was conceived to recede. Where luxury residential development frequently announces itself through surface and spectacle, the design direction at Virāma moves in the opposite register — materiality chosen for its aging properties as much as its appearance at handover, spatial planning that privileges threshold and approach over gross area, and a site arrangement that ensures no villa is incidental to the estate's overall composition. "[Architect Name] approached this as an exercise in restraint that never tips into austerity," the SSquare team noted during the design review. "Every decision had to hold its ground ten years from now, not just at the moment of completion." The result is a built environment that is quietly, durably itself — one that does not ask to be read at a glance.

## Developer Statement

"Virāma required us to make a choice that no commercial project ever demands: to decide, in advance, that we would stop. Not because the land runs out, or the market shifts, but because the promise we are making to these twelve families can only be kept at this scale. Estate management that does not drift, materials that are not substituted under cost pressure, a community that does not expand until its character dilutes — none of these hold at two hundred units. They hold at twelve. We have spent thirty-five years learning what it means to build something that lasts. Virāma is where we apply that entirely to the home."

— SSquare

## About SSquare

SSquare was established in 1991 and has spent three and a half decades delivering large-scale commercial real estate across India. The firm has completed in excess of ten million square feet of built space — office, retail, and institutional — for clients who demanded precision and reliability over speed. SSquare's track record is characterised not by volume of projects but by the consistency of its standards across them.

Virāma is SSquare's first residential project. The decision to enter the segment was made once — and for twelve families. The developer's approach to this launch reflects the same disposition that has governed its commercial work: that the standard, privately held, is the only one worth building to.
