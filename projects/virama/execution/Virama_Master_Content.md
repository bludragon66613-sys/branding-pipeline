## 1.1 BRAND POSITIONING STATEMENT

Virāma is not a reaction to the noise of Hyderabad's luxury market — it is indifferent to it. Twelve private villas set within a gated estate in the heart of Jubilee Hills, conceived for UHNI families who have already arrived and no longer need to announce it. Where other projects sell aspiration, Virāma sells stillness — a rare and finite commodity in one of India's fastest-moving cities. Backed by SSquare's 35-year track record in building to a standard it has never had to advertise, Virāma is the developer's first and only residential statement: architecture that breathes, land that holds, and a life that finally slows down.

---

## 1.2 BRAND VOICE RULES

**Rule 1 — The product does not need adjectives.**
If the word "luxury," "premium," or "world-class" appears in any copy, the line has failed. Describe what exists. What exists is sufficient.

**Rule 2 — Write for the person who has stopped being impressed.**
The audience has seen Amanresorts, owns homes in multiple cities, and has attended every Hyderabad launch. Speak to their fatigue, not their ambition. They want less, not more.

**Rule 3 — Short lines earn more trust than long ones.**
A sentence of twelve words carries more authority than a sentence of forty. Every headline should be able to stand alone in a half-page of white space. If it cannot hold that silence, rewrite it.

**Rule 4 — Specificity over grandeur.**
"A pivot door of solid teak" lands harder than "a dramatic entrance experience." The material is the statement. Name it, measure it, let it speak.

**Rule 5 — Never explain the brand — embody it.**
The copy does not describe serenity; it enacts serenity through rhythm, restraint, and negative space. If a line is working too hard, it is wrong.

## 1.3 AMENITY NARRATIVES

### The Still Water — Private Infinity Pool
Each villa at Virāma holds its own body of water — a private infinity pool set flush with the landscape, reflecting sky and canopy rather than a neighbouring balcony. This is not a shared amenity accessed by schedule. It is yours, always, at any hour, in complete silence. The water is the architecture.

### The Root System — Estate Garden & Landscape Corridors
The estate's landscape was not installed — it was inherited and deepened. Mature trees anchor every sightline, their canopies forming a living ceiling over gravel paths that connect villa to garden to courtyard in an unbroken chain of shade. Nothing here was placed to impress at a site visit. It was planted to grow for decades.

### The First Breath — Arrival Forecourt & Pivot Door Entrance
Before you enter the villa, the estate makes its intention clear. A motor court of honed limestone gives way to a pivot door — solid, slow, deliberate — that opens not with a push but with a presence. The arrival sequence was designed with a single brief: that the act of coming home should feel like a physical shedding of the outside world.

### The Gathering Stone — Common Pavilion & Resident Lounge
Twelve families. One pavilion. The common pavilion is not a clubhouse — it carries no gym equipment, no banquet hall, no LED walls. It is a sheltered stone terrace with deep overhangs, curated seating, and a view of the estate's central grove. A place to share a drink with neighbours you actually know, without an event management company arranging the moment.

### The Quiet Body — Wellness Courtyard
Set apart from the villa's main circulation, the wellness courtyard is enclosed on three sides by textured basalt walls and open on the fourth to an uninterrupted garden view. It holds space for morning practice, for stillness, for whatever discipline you bring to it. There is no equipment. There is no mirror. There is only light, air, and the particular quality of silence that comes from being surrounded by stone.

### The Green Circuit — Estate Walking Path
The Green Circuit is a 400-metre landscaped loop that links all twelve villas through the estate's living fabric — through grove, courtyard, garden, and back. It was named at the outset of the master plan and treated as infrastructure, not afterthought. Walk it once and you understand the estate's true scale. Walk it daily and it becomes the quiet ritual Virāma was built around.

## 1.5 DIFFERENTIATION CLAIMS

1. Virāma is the only gated villa estate in Jubilee Hills where no two homes share a wall, a view, or a pool. Privacy is not a feature here — it is the fundamental unit of the plan.

2. SSquare has delivered over ten million square feet of built space without ever entering the residential market. Virāma is the singular exception — which means twelve families are acquiring something SSquare will never replicate.

3. Every villa sits on a single storey. Not as a constraint, but as a conviction. Horizontal living on this land means the landscape is always in frame, the sky is always present, and you are never above or below anything.

4. The estate carries no commercial tenancy, no retail podium, no hotel-style lobby with strangers in transit. What passes through the gate belongs here. Everyone else does not.

5. The architecture references no local precedent. It was briefed against Aman's body of work — against Amanyara and Amangiri — not against what has been built in Hyderabad before. The result is a project that does not compete with its neighbours because it is not in the same conversation.

## 1.6 COMPETITOR CONTRAST

### vs. Prestige Glenwood, Jubilee Hills
Prestige Glenwood is a well-executed high-rise residential address — vertical, dense, appointment-driven. It performs luxury through height, view, and building specification. Virāma operates from the opposite conviction: that the most valuable thing SSquare can put on this land is not floors, but distance. The stillness of a private estate cannot be reproduced above the 20th storey, regardless of how well the lobby is finished.

### vs. My Home Avatar, Gachibowli
My Home Avatar delivers scale, amenity count, and clubhouse infrastructure at a tier that is genuinely impressive. It serves buyers who want a complete social ecosystem within their complex — and it does so with consistency. Virāma does not compete for that buyer. It is built for the family that has outgrown shared amenities, for whom the measure of a good home is no longer what the complex offers but how quiet the compound becomes after the gate closes at night.

### vs. Aparna Sarovar, Kondapur
Aparna's luxury offering delivers reliable execution within a familiar typology — mid-rise, club-amenity, gated community. It serves buyers who want luxury verified through a familiar checklist. Virāma makes no checklist argument. Its differentiation is architectural, not amenity-led — and it asks a buyer to read a building the way they would read a piece of considered design rather than evaluate a property on square footage and facilities per rupee. That is not a wider market. It is a more specific one.

## 2.1 PROJECT HERO NARRATIVE

There is a particular quality of silence that belongs only to certain places. Not the silence of emptiness — but the silence of arrival. Of finally being somewhere that asks nothing of you.

Virāma is twelve private villas set behind a single gate in Prashashan Nagar, Jubilee Hills. Ten minutes from HITEC City. A lifetime away from its noise.

The word itself is Sanskrit — विराम — a pause in speech, a moment held between breaths. Grammarians used it to mark the place where meaning settles before continuing. The name was chosen deliberately. This is not a residence that announces itself. It simply waits, with the patience of something built to last.

Each villa occupies its own world: single-storey, low to the ground, under a cantilevered roof that seems to float above walls of honed limestone and dark basalt. Teak pivot doors open onto interiors where the boundaries between inside and outside dissolve entirely. A private pool catches the afternoon light without ceremony. Mature trees — already old when most of Hyderabad was being built — filter the sky into something slower.

The neighbourhood carries a quiet reputation among those who know it: no through traffic, a deep canopy, the particular calm of a place that has never needed to prove itself. Families have raised children here. Gardens have grown in without instruction.

Behind Virāma is SSquare — thirty-five years building the commercial infrastructure of Hyderabad's ambitions: over ten million square feet of the city's most exacting commercial real estate, held privately, delivered without noise. This is their first residential project. It will, in all likelihood, be their only one.

Twelve villas. Twelve families. A community small enough to have character, private enough to remain yours.

*Come home to quiet.*

## 2.3 PRESS NOTE

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

### SSquare Announces Virāma: Twelve Private Villas in Jubilee Hills Marking the Group's Residential Debut After 35 Years in Commercial Real Estate

*The Hyderabad-based developer, responsible for over 10 million sq ft of the city's commercial infrastructure, enters the residential market with a strictly limited gated estate priced between Rs 13–20 Crore*

**Hyderabad, March 2026** — SSquare, one of Hyderabad's most enduring privately held real estate groups, has announced the launch of Virāma (विराम) — a gated estate of twelve single-storey private villas in Prashashan Nagar, Jubilee Hills. The project marks the group's first foray into residential real estate in its 35-year history, and by design, it is a considered one: twelve villas, no more.

Founded in 1991, SSquare has operated largely outside the noise of the mainstream real estate market, building a portfolio of over 10 million square feet of premium commercial real estate across Hyderabad, privately held and privately financed. The group's decision to enter the residential segment was not a strategic pivot so much as a personal one — an opportunity to apply three and a half decades of material knowledge, construction discipline, and architectural conviction to a product built for permanent occupation rather than commercial return.

Virāma takes its name from the Sanskrit grammatical term for pause — the mark placed at the end of a word to signal stillness before the next movement begins. The estate is designed around that premise. Situated in Prashashan Nagar, a neighbourhood locally known as "Malibu" for its insular character, mature tree canopy, and near-complete absence of through traffic, the project occupies a location that has resisted the pressures of rapid densification that have characterised much of Hyderabad's growth corridor. It sits ten minutes from HITEC City, but in an entirely different register.

Each of the twelve villas is single-storey, designed in the tradition of quiet modernism: honed limestone render against a dark basalt base, a cantilevered roof, floor-to-ceiling glazing that dissolves the boundary between interior and exterior, and a signature pivot door in teak. Every villa is accompanied by a private pool, set within the villa's own boundary. The architecture draws from a lineage that includes Aman's Amanyara resort in Turks and Caicos — buildings that achieve their effect through restraint rather than statement. The project is priced between Rs 13 and 20 Crore.

The target occupant is a family that has, in all probability, already lived in every other kind of luxury Hyderabad has to offer — and is now looking for something different. Not more. Different. Virāma was designed for buyers who understand that privacy is not an amenity that can be added to a building; it must be structural from the beginning.

*"SSquare has spent thirty-five years building the environments in which Hyderabad does its most consequential work. Virāma is the first time we have built for the people who do that work — for what their lives look like when the workday ends, and the gate closes behind them. We have approached it with the same standard we have held every commercial project to, and no greater fanfare. The product will speak for itself."*
