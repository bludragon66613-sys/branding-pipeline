# PART 1: THREE-PART TEASER HOARDING CAMPAIGN

## Campaign Architecture

Three hoardings in chronological sequence. They replace one another — they do not run simultaneously. The campaign is designed as a single, continuous sentence spoken in three chapters over twelve weeks. The visual language builds. The reveal is earned.

Each hoarding occupies the same physical locations. Continuity of placement is not incidental — it is the strategy. The same commuter sees the same board change. The mystery accumulates.

---

## HOARDING 01 — "THE TEASE"
### Weeks 1–4

---

### HEADLINE

> **Soon, twelve families will live in complete quiet.**

### ATTRIBUTION LINE
*(30% of headline point size)*

> Virāma. By SSquare.

---

### COPY RATIONALE

No promise is made. No address is given. No price, no product, no call to action. The sentence functions as a statement of quiet fact — as though the speaker already knows how this ends. "Twelve families" implies selection. "Complete quiet" implies rarity. The full stop is final. The word "Soon" is the only concession to time, and it implies that the wait is brief — that something has already been decided.

The attribution "Virāma. By SSquare." appears at 30% of headline size. It is not a logo line. It is a whisper after a sentence that did not need explaining.

---

### VISUAL DIRECTION

**Hero image:** Full-bleed extreme close-up of a still water surface. Photographed pre-dawn — 4:45–5:10 AM window — when ambient light is blue-grey and the water reads as near-monochrome. No sky. No horizon. No movement. The frame contains nothing but the surface of still water and the faint suggestion of depth below it.

**Colour palette:** Near-black at distance. Blue-grey at proximity. The image must read as almost black at 60 km/h from 40 metres. The headline must be the only legible element at speed.

**Atmosphere:** The quality of the photograph should suggest not a pool but a private body of water — the kind that belongs to a property that has never been described to strangers.

**Photography notes:**
- Shot on medium-format or full-frame at f/2.8–f/4 for maximum tonal depth
- No post-production vignette — the image itself provides the darkness
- Colour grade: desaturate to 15–20% saturation maximum. Slight blue-teal lift in shadows
- No reflections of sky visible in frame. The water faces nothing but itself

### PLACEMENT LOGIC

**Target arterials:**
1. Jubilee Hills Road No. 36 (primary — highest proximity to site)
2. Banjara Hills Road No. 10 (secondary — aspirational commuter corridor)
3. Film Nagar approach from Shaikpet (tertiary — UHNI residential feeder road)

**Placement criteria:**
- Minimum 10-second legibility window at 60 km/h (equivalent to 167m approach distance with unobstructed sightline)
- No competing hoarding within 100m on the same side of road
- Board faces oncoming traffic — not traffic departing the area
- Minimum 4 weeks in position before replacement with Hoarding 02

**Vendor brief note:** No bunting. No flags. No secondary panels. The board stands alone.

## HOARDING 02 — "THE STIR"
### Weeks 5–8

---

### HEADLINE

> **Twelve families. Twelve villas. One address that will never be repeated.**

### SUB-ATTRIBUTION LINE
*(25% of headline point size)*

> Virāma. By SSquare. Coming [Month] 2026.

---

### COPY RATIONALE

The sentence structure mirrors Hoarding 01 — short declarative clauses, building rhythm. "Twelve families. Twelve villas." is almost percussive — the repetition of twelve emphasises scarcity without stating it as a sales argument. "One address that will never be repeated" is the turn: it introduces finality. Not urgency. Finality is more powerful — urgency can be manufactured, finality cannot.

The month appears now. Not the date. The month is a narrowing. It creates a calendar in the reader's mind. They begin to hold a date.

---

### VISUAL DIRECTION

**Hero image:** The same body of water — but the camera has pulled back. The frame now reveals one element of context: a stone coping edge at the lower-left corner of the frame, entering the image at approximately 15% of board width. In the far distance — far enough that it reads as a silhouette, not a landscape feature — a single cypress. The horizon is implied but remains out of frame.

The water still dominates. The project is not shown. But the world around the water has begun to exist.

**Photography notes:**
- Ideally the same shoot as Hoarding 01 — simply a different frame pulled from the same session to preserve tonal continuity
- Same colour grade. Same pre-dawn blue-grey
- The cypress must be far enough that it creates verticality without becoming identifiable as a garden feature
- The stone edge must be warm — limestone or travertine — enough to read as architectural, not industrial

---

### DESIGN SPECIFICATION

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| Format | 20ft × 10ft — identical to Hoarding 01 |
| Substrate | UV-resistant flex or ACP — identical to Hoarding 01 |
| Background | Full-bleed photography — same session, wider frame |
| Typography | Identical to Hoarding 01 — no variation in typeface, size ratio, or colour |
| Headline position | Centred. Two lines. Same vertical position as Hoarding 01 for visual continuity across the replacement sequence |
| Sub-attribution | Same typeface, 25% of headline size. "[Month] 2026" in standard numeral format, not spelled out |
| RERA number | Appears for the first time. Positioned at foot of board, centred. 8pt equivalent at full scale. Tracking: +120. Opacity: 50%. Copy: "RERA Reg. No. [XXXX] \| MahaRERA/TS-RERA as applicable" |
| Contact details | None |
| QR code | None |
| URL | None |

**Production note:** The RERA number at 50% opacity is a deliberate design choice, not an oversight. It fulfils regulatory requirement while maintaining the visual silence of the board. Confirm with legal team that 50% opacity on white text on dark background meets RERA legibility standards in Telangana. If not, increase to 70% opacity.

**Placement:** Direct replacement at the same three sites as Hoarding 01. The physical location does not change. The continuity of site is part of the campaign logic — the same commuter sees the board evolve.

## HOARDING 03 — "THE INVITATION"
### Weeks 9–12 / Through Launch

---

### HEADLINE COPY — SPLIT LAYOUT

**LEFT PANEL (60% of board width):**

> **The last twelve villas will always be the ones someone else didn't wait for.**

**RIGHT PANEL (40% of board width):**

*(Fine serif, 40% of headline size, right-aligned within panel)*

> virama.in

*(2 line-breaks)*

> SSquare — The Standard, Privately Held.

---

### COPY RATIONALE

The left panel headline is the only moment in the campaign where consequence is implied. Not urgency — consequence. "The last twelve villas" assumes ownership: they are already spoken of as belonging to someone. "Will always be" introduces permanence — this is a statement about the future made with certainty. "Someone else didn't wait for" is the turn that is not a threat. It is a quiet observation. The reader inserts themselves into the sentence.

The right panel establishes the destination: virama.in. The line "SSquare — The Standard, Privately Held." is not a tagline. It is a credential stated without emphasis. The comma is load-bearing — it separates the name from what the name means.

---

### VISUAL DIRECTION

**Hero image:** Full-bleed architectural photography of a single villa facade. The frame shows one elevation — front-facing — in golden-hour light. Pale lime plaster or stone-finished render, warm in the raking light. Dark basalt threshold: the step, the doorframe, the shadow line at the entrance. The door itself may be partially visible — dark timber, slightly recessed. No handles visible if possible.

**Exclusions (mandatory):**
- No people in frame
- No staging furniture visible (no poolside loungers, no decorative objects placed for photography)
- No car in driveway or on approach
- No neighbouring villas visible
- No sky-hung banners or site hoardings visible in background
- No construction equipment or temporary fencing

**Light:** Golden hour — 30–45 minutes before sunset. The light rakes horizontally across the facade, emphasising texture. The threshold is in shadow. The upper face of the structure catches full warm light.

**Mood:** The architecture speaks for itself. The photograph should feel as though you stumbled upon it — not as though it was composed.

---

### DESIGN SPECIFICATION

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| Format | 20ft × 10ft — identical to Hoardings 01 and 02 |
| Substrate | ACP preferred for Hoarding 03 — greater rigidity for the launch phase |
| Background | Full-bleed architectural photography |
| Layout | Vertical split: Left panel 60%, right panel 40% |
| Left panel | Headline text only. Freight Display, white, flush-left within left panel with 8% margin each side. Two–three lines depending on final point size testing |
| Right panel | URL and developer credit. Fine serif (Cormorant Garamond or equivalent), right-aligned. 40% of headline point size |
| Panel division | No visible dividing line. Panels defined purely by copy positioning |
| RERA number | Required for sales communication. Positioned at foot of board, spanning full width. Centred. 10pt equivalent at full scale. White. 70% opacity. Full RERA registration text as required by Telangana RERA |
| QR code | Bottom-right corner, right panel. 120mm × 120mm at full scale. White QR on dark overlay (20% black). Links to virama.in/enquire. No "scan me" instruction |
| URL | virama.in — appears in right panel copy and encoded in QR |

**QR code specification:**
- Error correction level: H (30%) — ensures legibility even if substrate has minor surface variation
- Quiet zone: minimum 4 modules on all sides
- Tested at distance: confirm scan-ability from 3 metres before printing

## WHATSAPP CREATIVE IMAGE BRIEF

**Format:** 1200 × 628px (landscape — WhatsApp link preview dimensions)
**Resolution:** 72 DPI screen-optimised, supplied also at 144 DPI for retina displays
**Colour profile:** sRGB

**Compositional elements — in order of visual hierarchy:**

1. **Background:** Virāma Stone — a custom off-white with warm grey undertone. Not pure white. Hex reference: approximately #F2EDE8. This is the brand's ground colour. Matte, unlit, like the surface of good paper.

2. **Virāma wordmark:** Centred. Freight Display or equivalent didone serif. Colour: Deep charcoal — #1A1A18. Size: approximately 14% of image height. Letterspace: +40 tracking. The wordmark carries the silence of the brand.

3. **Tagline:** "Come Home to Quiet." — positioned below wordmark with breathing room (minimum 24px gap at final resolution). Same typeface, lighter weight (Freight Display Book or equivalent). Size: 38% of wordmark size. Colour: Arcadian Gold — #B89A5A or closest PMS equivalent. Do not italicise.

4. **Horizontal rule:** Single hairline rule between tagline and event information. 40% of image width, centred. Colour: Arcadian Gold, 60% opacity. Stroke weight: 1px at 144 DPI.

5. **Event information:** Below the rule. Inter 400 (Regular), 12px at 72 DPI. Colour: #1A1A18. Two lines maximum:
   - Line 1: [Day], [Date] — [Time]
   - Line 2: By Invitation Only

**Exclusions (mandatory):**
- No price on image
- No "12 villas only" or equivalent scarcity device
- No urgency markers of any kind
- No additional photography or illustrative elements
- No developer logo beyond the wordmark if Virāma is treated as the primary brand identity
- No RERA number on the WhatsApp image (messaging context, not sales collateral)

**Restraint note for design team:** The image will appear as a link preview thumbnail. Its job is not to explain. Its job is to be unmistakably different from every other real estate message the recipient has received that day. Silence is the strategy.

## PHYSICAL INVITE SPECIFICATION

---

### Outer Envelope

**Stock:** Recycled kraft, natural brown. Uncoated. Weight: 140gsm minimum.
**Size:** C5 (162mm × 229mm) — fits A5 card without folding
**Front face:** Debossed "V" monogram only. The Virāma monogram — not the full wordmark, not "Virāma" spelled out. Centred at upper third of envelope face. Deboss depth: 0.8–1.0mm. No ink fill. The mark is felt, not read.
**Reverse:** Gummed flap. No printed content. No return address on envelope face.
**Addressing:** Hand-addressed in black ink by a calligrapher. No printed labels. No printed names on the front of the envelope under any circumstances.
**Sealing:** Wax seal on reverse flap. Seal die: the S² monogram. Wax colour: deep charcoal or matte black — not red, not gold. The gold is reserved for the interior.

---

### Inner Card

**Format:** A5 portrait — 148mm × 210mm
**Stock:** Fedrigoni Tintoretto Gesso 350gsm. If unavailable: Gmund Cotton 350gsm or Arjowiggins Conqueror Wove 350gsm. No substitution with gloss or silk-coated stock under any circumstances.
**Finish:** Matte. Tactile. The paper must feel deliberate in the hand.

**Front face (exterior, facing out from envelope):**
The Virāma wordmark — blind embossed. No ink. No foil. No other content.
Emboss specification: deep relief, 1.2–1.5mm. Male/female die on letterpress or foil-blocking press. The name is raised from the surface of the card. It is felt before it is read.
**That is all on the front face.** No strapline. No developer name. No date. Silence.

### RSVP Card

**Format:** 100mm × 60mm
**Stock:** Same Fedrigoni Tintoretto Gesso — 350gsm. Cut from the same batch as the inner card.
**Front face:**

```
I will attend.

Name: _________________________________
```

Typography: Freight Display Pro, 10pt. "I will attend." in regular weight. "Name:" in same face, lighter weight. The rule: 80mm, hairline, 0.25pt.

No checkboxes. No "number of guests." No RSVP-by date on the card face. The card itself implies urgency without stating it.

**Reverse face:**
Return address only. Centred. Inter 400, 7pt, tracking +80:

```
SSquare
[Office Address]
Hyderabad — [PIN]
```

No logo on reverse. No "please post by" date. No postage paid mark if hand-delivery is preferred — confirm with the SSquare team whether RSVP is by post, WhatsApp confirmation, or dedicated phone number.

### Print Production Notes

**Preferred process:** Letterpress for all text elements. Letterpress achieves a depth of impression — a physical indentation in the paper — that offset and digital cannot replicate. The recipient must feel that this card was made for them specifically, even if 40 were printed.

**If letterpress is unavailable at required quantity:** Deep foil-blocking on text elements (letterpress alternative) with engraving for the blind emboss on the front face.

**Foil application:** Arcadian Gold foil on the S² monogram only — not on the Virāma wordmark, which remains blind-embossed in the paper. The foil appears once. On the interior — bottom of right page or as part of the wordmark's registration mark. Confirm position with design director before going to press.

**Proofing requirements:**
- One letterpress proof of front face blind emboss — confirm depth and crispness before full run
- One full spread proof with all copy in position — confirm leading and margins at actual scale
- One RSVP card proof — confirm rule alignment and reverse address legibility

**Quantity:** Print 60 sets (40 for invitees, 10 for developer records, 10 for damage/error contingency). Do not print more than 60. The scarcity of the object is part of the brand experience.

**Packaging for dispatch:** Each invitation packaged in a single-piece tissue wrap — no printed tissue, no branded tissue — before being placed in the envelope. No padding. The card should feel substantial on its own.

**Courier:** Hand-delivered by a uniformed courier if within Hyderabad metropolitan area. No post-box delivery. If recipient is not present, card is returned and a second delivery is arranged. The invitation does not sit in a letterbox.

## BRAND CONSISTENCY NOTES FOR ALL VENDORS

1. The brand voice is the same across every touchpoint — hoarding, WhatsApp message, invitation, interior card copy. Nothing shouts. Nothing repeats itself. Nothing explains what does not require explanation.

2. The word "luxury" does not appear anywhere in this campaign. The word "exclusive" does not appear. The word "prestigious" does not appear. These words have been used too many times by too many projects that had to say so.

3. The number twelve is not used as a scarcity device. It is used as a fact. The reader is trusted to understand the implication without being told.

4. The brand typeface (Freight Display or equivalent didone serif) must be consistent across every printed and digital touchpoint. No substitution for system fonts in any WhatsApp creative or digital asset.

5. Virāma is spelled with the macron — Virāma — wherever typographic control is available. In contexts where the macron cannot be guaranteed (SMS, plain-text messaging), Virama is acceptable. The macron is always preferred.
