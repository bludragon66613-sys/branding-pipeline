The building that refracts
light as intention.
In 2024, Binghatti Developers and Jacob & Co produced the Binghatti Phantom — a 104-storey tower whose facade is modelled after a diamond crystal structure. Every face cut at a precise angle so the building refracts light differently at dawn, noon, dusk, and night. Jacob & Co's DNA is the movement-as-artwork: the tourbillon visible through the dial, mechanics displayed as beauty. Binghatti's DNA is kinetic geometry: bold, angular, buildings that announce themselves before you arrive.

The Phantom is not subtle. It was never trying to be. It was trying to be the most extraordinary object in the world's most extraordinary city — and it succeeded because every design decision began with that intention.
The Translation
"A building should announce your arrival before you open the door."
Revel in Jubilee Hills does not reproduce the Phantom's scale or its Dubai context. It takes its founding instinct: that architecture should be an event. That arriving home should feel like arriving somewhere. That a building which knows what it is communicates that immediately — in its facade geometry, its entrance sequence, its relationship to light. Revel is the only address in Hyderabad where the act of arriving is itself designed.
The Revel System
Four Defining Elements
01
The Cut
Facade
Every plane of the villa's exterior facade is angled. Not for decoration — for light capture. The geometry is calculated so that no two moments of day produce the same visual surface. At 7am the east face is pure illumination. By noon the upper planes divide into shadow and reflection. At dusk the bronze-toned cladding catches the hour like a dial registers a complication.
02
Refractive
Glass
Glazing specified not for transparency alone but for its angular response to direct light. The Hyderabad sun is strong and specific — arriving early, departing late, at a low angle most of the year. Revel's glazing system responds to this: the glass moves the light around the interior rather than admitting it bluntly. The effect is a room that changes colour on its own schedule.
03
Bronze
Reveal
Every window, every door frame, every architectural joint is finished in a warm bronze reveal — a fine line of metal that catches direct light and throws it back with a different temperature. The bronze does not dominate. It articulates. The building reads as monolithic at distance; at proximity it reveals the precision of its detailing. This is how Jacob & Co builds a watch. This is how Revel builds a wall.
04
The Arrival
Axis
The motor court is not a driveway. It is a choreographed approach sequence: gate, forecourt, the building reveals itself at a calculated angle — never full-face until you are within it. You see the facade in stages, each a different cut of light. The path from gate to front door is a designed experience. Arrival here is not the beginning of being home. It is itself the event.
Each element of the Revel design system originates in the same logic as the Binghatti Phantom: that bold geometric intention, precisely executed, produces architecture that cannot be unseen — and therefore cannot be forgotten.

Light as Architecture
The building
as instrument
of light.
Every angle of Revel's facade was calculated to respond to the Hyderabad morning light differently. The east-facing planes catch the early sun at a specific refraction angle. The bronze reveals redirect it. The cut glazing diffuses it. By 8am, the building is performing — and it does this every day, without instruction, because the geometry is fixed. This is not passive architecture. It is an instrument tuned to a specific sky.
The Design Principle
Not where you live quietly — where you arrive with intention.
Jacob & Co makes watches where the movement is visible through the dial because they believe the mechanism is itself beautiful. Binghatti designed a tower where the facade geometry changes its appearance through the day because they believe a building should perform. Revel applies this to residential architecture in Jubilee Hills: the villa does not sit passively at its address. It responds to the sky above it, to the position of the sun, to the angle of approach. Every morning it is slightly different. Every morning it is yours. This is not a residence. It is an object of precision in which you happen to live — and you will never mistake it for anything else.

Jubilee Hills built its reputation slowly, over decades, through the cumulative presence of families who did not need to be elsewhere. Prashashan Nagar is its most settled pocket — the tree canopy arrived without being planted, the streets acquired their quietness without being enforced. It is everything Dubai's Marina is not: unhurried, rooted, already complete.

Revel does not try to match that register. It introduces a different note into the neighbourhood — precision, geometry, the forward-facing energy of a building that knows exactly what it is. Jubilee Hills has been many things. It has been old money, new confidence, and quiet inheritance. Revel is its next statement: that the address accommodates ambition as easily as it accommodates history. The twelve families who arrive here will not blend into the neighbourhood. They will add to it.
